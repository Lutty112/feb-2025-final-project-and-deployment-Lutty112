Why Monitor Water Resources?

Water is a finite resource facing increasing pressure due to population growth, pollution, and climate change. Monitoring both the quantity and quality of water is essential to ensure sustainable use and availability for future generations. With GIS and Remote Sensing technologies, environmental experts can gather accurate, real-time data to analyze water trends, detect pollution sources, and model hydrological systems effectively.

By using satellite imagery, sensors, and spatial analysis tools, professionals can monitor lakes, rivers, reservoirs, and aquifers. These tools help identify areas of water stress, map flooding zones, and assess the health of aquatic ecosystems.
